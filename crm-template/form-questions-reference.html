How your forms connect to the CRM

Your Google Form is the entry point for Path A leads - anyone who opts in for a freebie or submits an enquiry. When they submit, their details are automatically added to your Google Sheet, which triggers the 5-email Apps Script sequence.

The questions below determine what data lands in your sheet. Every question in your form becomes a column. Keep it focused - only ask what you will actually use. More questions = more friction = fewer submissions.

Form 2 - Post-Purchase
New Client Intake
Sent after payment is confirmed - before the first session. This does not trigger the email sequence. It fills in the detail you need to work well with this person.
Question Field type Status Notes
Date of birth Date Recommended Useful if your work draws on astrology, human design, or age-related considerations. Remove if not relevant to your practice.
Identifiers and pronouns Short text Recommended Shows care and avoids misgendering in written and verbal communication.

What days generally work best for you? Paragraph Recommended Prevents scheduling back-and-forth. Useful for any ongoing work.
What time of day are you most available? Checkboxes Recommended Checkboxes work well here. Suggested options: Morning, Midday, Afternoon, Evening.
Any days or times that are completely off limits? Short text Optional Catches edge cases that the availability question might miss - school pickups, recurring commitments, etc.
What are you hoping to get from our work together? Paragraph Optional Useful context before the first session. Helps you prepare and shows the client you're already thinking about them.

Tip: Send this form as a link in the payment confirmation email or via a follow-up message. Don't include it in the booking flow itself - keep purchase friction low and collect details after the commitment is made.

Setup Notes
Building Your Forms in Google Forms
Quick reminders for getting the forms working correctly with the rest of the CRM.
Link Form 1 to your Sheet In Google Forms - go to Responses tab - click the Sheets icon. Link to your existing CRM sheet (don't create a new one). This is what triggers the Apps Script sequence.
Column order matters The Apps Script reads specific column positions (A = name, B = email etc). If you add questions to Form 1, add them after column E so they don't break the sequence trigger.
Form 2 and 3 are standalone These don't need to connect to the main CRM sheet. You can link them to a separate tab or a separate sheet - just somewhere you can refer back to before sessions.
Update dropdowns when services change Whenever you add, remove, or rename a service, update the dropdown in Form 1. Stale options in the sheet make filtering harder over time.
Test before going live Submit a test entry using your own email. Check the sheet row appeared correctly and that Email 1 arrived within a few minutes. If it didn't, check the Apps Script trigger is set to "On form submit".
