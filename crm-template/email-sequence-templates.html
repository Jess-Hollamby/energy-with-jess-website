Before you start

How this sequence works

Emails 1, 2, 3 - Automated Sent by Apps Script on Day 0, 3, and 7. Paste the final copy into the script where marked. These send without you doing anything.
Emails 4, 5 - Manual drafts Apps Script creates a draft in Gmail. You review, personalise for this specific person, and hit send when ready. Don't send these word for word - make them feel personal.
Write in your voice These templates are a starting point. Change any phrasing that doesn't sound like you. The structure matters more than the exact words.
Keep it short None of these emails need to be long. A warm, genuine 5-sentence email beats a formal 3-paragraph one every time.

Email body
Hi [First Name], Welcome - I'm so glad you're here. Here's your [FREEBIE NAME]: [LINK] [1-2 sentences about what's in it and how to use it. Keep it warm and practical, not salesy.] I'm [YOUR NAME] - I'm a [WHAT YOU DO, e.g. energy healer and mentor] and I help [WHO YOU HELP] [what you help them do/feel]. Over the next little while I'll be sharing [what kind of content they can expect - e.g. tips, insights, honest reflections]. Nothing overwhelming - just things that might actually be useful. If you have any questions or want to reach out, just reply to this email. [Warm sign-off], [YOUR NAME] [BUSINESS NAME] [WEBSITE]
Tips for this email
  • Deliver the freebie in the first few lines - don't make them scroll for it
  • Keep the intro short - they signed up for the freebie, not a bio
  • Set expectations gently - tell them you'll be in touch, so your next emails don't feel random

Tips for this email
  • Short is better here - 4 to 6 sentences is plenty
  • Ask one question only - makes it easy to reply
  • No CTA or link needed - this one is purely relational

Value and Soft Offer

Day 7 - give something useful, then gently introduce a next step

Auto
Subject line options - pick one
Something that might help
One thing I keep coming back to
Email body
Hi [First Name], [Open with a short piece of genuine value - a tip, a perspective shift, a common thing you see, or a question worth sitting with. 3-5 sentences. Make it useful on its own, not a lead-in to a pitch.] [Optional: one sentence that bridges from the value to the person reading - e.g. "If any of that resonates, it might be worth exploring further."] If you're curious about working together, [describe what the first step is - e.g. I offer a free 30-minute discovery call / you can book a session directly] - [LINK]. No pressure at all - just here if it's the right time. [Warm sign-off], [YOUR NAME]
Tips for this email
  • Lead with the value - the offer should feel like an afterthought, not the point
  • One link only - to a booking page, discovery call, or your main service
  • "No pressure" language works well here - it reduces resistance without being weak

Personal Check-in

Day 14 - a draft is created in Gmail for you to review and personalise before sending

Manual
Subject line options - pick one
Hi [First Name] - just thinking of you
Still here if you need anything
Draft structure - personalise this before sending
Hi [First Name], [Open with something personal or specific to this person if you can - even a small detail from what you know about them, or something you've noticed about people in their situation. If you have nothing specific, a warm general opener is fine.] [1-2 sentences. A genuine question, a soft offer, or simply acknowledging where they might be right now. This email should feel like it was written just for them - because it should be.] I'm here if anything's come up or if you have questions. You can always reply to this or [mention another way to reach you if relevant - e.g. book a free call / message me directly]. [Warm sign-off], [YOUR NAME]
Tips for this email
  • This one must feel personal - rewrite the draft before you send it, don't just swap the name
  • If you remember anything from their signup (e.g. what they selected), reference it
  • No hard sell - this is about keeping the relationship warm
  • If they've already replied or booked, you can skip this email for them

Final Touchpoint

Day 30 - last email in the sequence, keep the door open without pressure

Manual
Subject line options - pick one
Before I go quiet...
Leaving the door open
Draft structure - personalise this before sending
Hi [First Name], This is the last email in my welcome sequence - after this I won't be showing up in your inbox unless you reach out. [1-2 sentences. Acknowledge that timing matters. Some people aren't ready now but will be later - this email is for them. Keep it genuinely warm, not salesy.] If you ever want to [describe what working together looks like at the simplest entry point - e.g. start with a reading / book a free call / explore what's possible], I'm here. [LINK or contact details] Wishing you well either way. [Warm sign-off], [YOUR NAME]
Tips for this email
  • Name the fact that it's the last email - it creates honesty and often prompts a reply
  • "Leaving the door open" is the entire job of this email - not converting, just staying warm
  • After this, they stay in your sheet - you can follow up manually any time you like
  • People who don't respond now often come back months later - this email plants that seed
