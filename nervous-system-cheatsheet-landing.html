Which one keeps showing up for you?

Fight mode You snap at people you love over tiny things. You feel like you're constantly braced for an argument that hasn't happened yet.
Flight mode You're always doing, always busy, never able to sit still - but underneath all the productivity, something feels like it's running away.
Fawn mode You say yes when you mean no. You read every room before you speak. You've lost track of where other people end and you begin.
Freeze mode You feel stuck. Numb. Like you're watching your life from the outside. You know something needs to shift but you can't find the door.

None of that is a character flaw.
It's a pattern - and once you can see it, you can work with it.

What's inside

Four modes. One nervous system.
Which one do you recognise?

Fight mode

Fight

Anger, explosions, control, slamming doors - your body believes there's a threat and it's gearing up to face it.

Regulation tools include Cold water therapy, high intensity movement, shaking, scream therapy, journaling the trigger
Flight mode

Flight

Overthinking, perfectionism, workaholism, panic - your body believes the safest thing is to keep moving and never stop.

Regulation tools include Deep belly breathing, power walking, grounding, yin yoga, hands on body
Fawn mode

Fawn

People-pleasing, no boundaries, self-critique, codependency - your body learned that keeping others happy kept you safe.

Regulation tools include Setting and enforcing boundaries, shaking, checking in with your own emotions first
Freeze mode

Freeze

Numb, stuck, dissociated, exhausted, can't decide - your body has shut down because it ran out of other options.

Regulation tools include Diaphragmatic breathing, full body check-in, singing and dancing, self massage, yin yoga
